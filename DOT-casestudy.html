Department of Transportation

Government Website Redesign Case Study

Parti / D.O.T. / ASF

This web case study was an individual project constructed to help get a better understanding of the UX/UI design process through a 5 step process: Research, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test. I put an emphasis on Navigation UI and button states throughout the project to get a better understanding of how a site should flow page-to-page.

My Role:

UX Researcher, UX Designer, UI Designer

Research

User Persona

I started my research process by attempting to understand my user. I initially struggled with defining the needs and goals of my user since the main purpose of the site is to provide a wide variety of information on transportation at a national scale.

Settling on Dereck, who is interested in learning more about motorcycle safety and receiving updates on public transportation projects in his city.

User Path & Hueristic Evaluation

My next step was evaluating the current website, and how my user would navigate throughout in order to find the information he is looking for. I defined the user path, and then conducted a Heuristic Evaluation of the site to organize what I believed should be changed to make the experience easier and more enjoyable.

Usability Testing

To solidify the research and evaluation I had just done, I arranged a handful of user tests to see what issues other people face when navigating D.O.T.â€™s website. Having different multiple examples not only helped prove what problems I had already found, but also brought up things that I had not noticed during my own tests.

Ideation

Sitemap & Navigation UI

After evaluating and redlining the current D.O.T. website, I transitioned into redesigning the navigation. I started this process by cardsorting the current navigation features and then taking that information to form a new structure and build a sitemap.

This allowed me to simplify the new Navigation UI and build a Low-Fidelity wireframe for the menu navigation.

UI Style Tile

Moving into the Hi-Fidelity prototype, I started by pulling images to inspire the Style Guide. This guide includes everything from typography, logos, and color palettes in order to reference when building the Hi-Fidelity prototypes.

Many of the style decisions I made were influenced by a feeling of Innovation, Movement, and Energy. I wanted to keep the primary color of blue that D.O.T. has currently, but defined the secondary color of orange as it is a complimentary color and exudes a sense of movement to relate to transportation.
